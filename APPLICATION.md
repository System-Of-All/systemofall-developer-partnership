# Application to the SALL Token Partnership Program

The SALL Token Partnership Program welcomes **builders, researchers, and specialists** who are ready to contribute to the ecosystem. This guide explains how to apply and what information is required.



## Step 1 – Identify Your Contribution Track
Review [CONTRIBUTION_TRACKS.md](CONTRIBUTION_TRACKS.md) and determine the track(s) where you can make the most meaningful contribution:  

- **Technical**
- **Research**
- **Governance**

You may apply for one or multiple tracks depending on your expertise.



## Step 2 – Review Partnership Tiers
Review [PARTNERSHIP_TIERS.md](PARTNERSHIP_TIERS.md) to identify the tier that best aligns with your expected role, scope, and level of engagement:  

- **Tier 1 – Core Contributor**  
- **Tier 2 – Specialist / Advisor**  
- **Tier 3 – Infrastructure Partner**

Your tier will guide the evaluation of your contribution and potential token allocation.



## Step 3 – Prepare Your Portfolio
Provide a brief overview of your qualifications and experience relevant to your selected track(s) and tier. This can include:

- Resume or CV highlighting technical, research, or governance achievements
- GitHub or project links demonstrating prior contributions
- Publications, technical papers, or research outputs
- Examples of milestone-driven work or deployments



## Step 4 – Submit Your Application
Send your application to the SALL Token Partnership Program team via one of the following channels:

- **Email:** [development@systemofall.com](mailto:development@systemofall.com)  
- **Telegram:** [t.me/systemofall](https://t.me/systemofall)



## Evaluation Criteria
Applications are **evaluated based on capability, relevance, and measurable contribution**:

- Alignment with ecosystem architecture and priorities
- Depth of expertise in chosen track(s)
- Demonstrated track record of execution and delivery
- Ability to collaborate with other contributors professionally

> Note: Submission does not guarantee acceptance. Participation is merit-based and contribution-driven.
